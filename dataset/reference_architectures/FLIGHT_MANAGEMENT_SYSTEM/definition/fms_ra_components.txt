FPL & Navigation: Manages flight plan inputs and navigation data. It provides route information to downstream guidance and trajectory modules.

Guidance: Translates planned trajectories into steering commands. It ensures the vehicle follows the intended path during flight.

Path Monitor: Observes current trajectory execution and predicts future motion. It detects deviations and provides feedback for corrective actions.

Path Correction: Adjusts the reference path when deviations or conflicts are detected. It refines the trajectory to maintain safety and compliance.

Negotiation: Handles constraint and intent exchange with external entities. It updates operational limits based on predicted motion and system objectives.

Trajectory Prediction: Estimates future vehicle motion based on current state, intent, and constraints. It supports monitoring, correction, and optimization processes.

UAV Avoidance: Detects potential conflicts with other airspace users. It provides avoidance inputs to influence trajectory optimization.

Cost Function: Evaluates candidate trajectories using performance, safety, and efficiency criteria. It assigns optimization metrics for trajectory selection.

Trajectory Optimization: Computes an optimal trajectory under given constraints and cost metrics. It balances safety, performance, and operational objectives.

Performance Management: Monitors and manages vehicle performance parameters. It ensures compliance with operational limits and efficiency targets.

Constraints: Defines operational, physical, and regulatory limits. It restricts trajectory optimization and decision-making processes.

Model: Represents the vehicle’s dynamic and performance characteristics. It provides predictive capability for constraint evaluation and optimization.

Databases: Store flight data, environmental information, and operational parameters. They provide reference data for planning, prediction, and optimization.
